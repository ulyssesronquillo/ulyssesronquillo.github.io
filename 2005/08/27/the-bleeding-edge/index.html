The Bleeding Edge

Aug 27, 2005 • ulysses

Well, after a couple of hours fighting Gallery, I managed to get it back online yesterday. I upgraded Gallery several weeks ago, but I didn’t bother checking how it looked like on Internet Explorer. The menu border was missing and the fonts were out of whack. How can Gallery looks so good in Firefox and be horrible in IE? Are the two browsers really that far apart? Something’s wrong. I checked the CSS file to see if it needed tweaking. No luck there. It dawned on me. It was the Gallery upgrade several weeks ago.

So, I backtracked to an older version of Gallery. I used FTP to upload the files, but it didn’t work. I think my FTP client ignores writing over newer files. As a result, my Gallery files were completely out of whack. I ended up with a hodgepodge or multiple versions of Gallery in one directory. I ended up deleting everything except for the albums folder. Just a word of caution: don’t delete the albums folder or say goodbye to your pictures. After a couple of hours tweaking Gallery, I managed to get it working again. I checked how it looked like in IE. So far, so good.

Then comes the customization part. I added a html wrap to both header and footer, so that Gallery will look like it’s part of my current theme in WordPress. I checked Internet Explorer once again and wala, everything is working once again.

Lessons learned.

  1. Always check cross functionality between browsers: Firefox, IE, etc.
  2. Delete everything when downgrading software, but backup the important files.
  3. If it aint broke, don’t mess around with it. A minor upgrade turned out to be several hours of work.
